# User Testing Report — Milloy Web Studio

**Tester:** Sister (family member, non-technical user)
**Sites tested:** Bill & Bea's Drive-In, Emerald Edge Barber Shop, Milloy Web Studio Portfolio
**Testing method:** Walk-through observation with structured questions

---

## Observation Notes

### Bill & Bea's Drive-In
- No stumbling or confusion during navigation
- Browsed through menu sections naturally
- Noticed Dogs and Soups being grouped together felt odd
- Found the extras/sauces location slightly confusing relative to menu items
- Noted one image could be improved

### Emerald Edge Barber Shop
- No navigation difficulties
- Moved through site without assistance
- Noticed lack of storefront photos immediately
- Found the booking buttons clear and plentiful

### Milloy Web Studio Portfolio
- Text blending in one description section made it hard to read
- Client site images appeared slightly bugged
- Email link not functioning correctly
- Otherwise navigated the layout well

---

## Question 1 — How well did this solve your problem?

**Bill & Bea's:** Really good. Many websites don't show food options or prices. Having a full menu with categories, prices, and a map that opens to directions was genuinely helpful and solves the core problem well.

**Emerald Edge:** Strong. The service packages clearly list what's included and how long each service takes. The color scheme is clean and the cancellation policy is clearly visible which builds trust.

**Portfolio:** Laid out well. Shows off completed work effectively. If someone wanted to book web design services they could understand the process and see real examples of finished sites.

---

## Question 2 — What was difficult to understand?

**All three sites:** Nothing was difficult to understand. Navigation was easy across all three, the nav bar worked well on every site, and no real challenges were encountered during the walkthrough.

---

## Question 3 — What worked well?

**Bill & Bea's:** Photos and the nav bar. Being able to jump to each section quickly made it easy to find information. The menu layout with categories worked well.

**Emerald Edge:** The booking buttons are prominent and easy to find. Having multiple clear calls to action throughout the page made it obvious how to take the next step.

**Portfolio:** The overall layout and how completed client work is displayed. The process section explaining how the web design service works was clear and helpful for a potential client evaluating whether to hire.

---

## Question 4 — What features would you like to see added?

**Bill & Bea's:** No major changes. Possibly swap one image for a better one.

**Emerald Edge:** Add a photo of the actual storefront so visitors can visualize the physical location before visiting. More photos overall would significantly improve the site.

**Portfolio:** Add a contact section or form so interested visitors can reach out directly. Fix the spacing of text in descriptions.

---

## Question 5 — If you could change one thing, what would it be?

**Bill & Bea's:** Swap out one image for something better.

**Emerald Edge:** Add storefront photos and fix the Google Maps embed.

**Portfolio:** Fix the wording and spacing in the description sections to make them read more clearly.

---

## Overall Results Summary

| Area | Result |
|---|---|
| Navigation | Passed on all three sites — no confusion, no dead ends |
| Content clarity | Strong on Bill & Bea's and Emerald Edge — portfolio needs work |
| Visual quality | Bill & Bea's and portfolio solid — Emerald Edge held back by lack of photos |
| Functionality | Broken email link and bugged client images on portfolio need immediate fixes |

**Biggest strengths:** Menu with prices on Bill & Bea's, service packages on Emerald Edge, layout and case studies on portfolio.

**Biggest weaknesses:** Lack of storefront imagery on Emerald Edge, broken email link and text spacing on portfolio.
